EC1730 - Career Presentations & Interview Scheduling 
?? Lesson Aim
Equip learners to:
* Deliver a concise 2–3 minute career micro-presentation.
* Practice professional outreach and interview scheduling.
* Secure a practitioner interview or petition a mini-case.
* Submit acceptable scheduling evidence and maintain an Outreach Log.

?? Learning Outcomes
By the end of the session, learners will be able to:
* ?? Deliver a structured 2–3 minute micro-presentation highlighting H&S relevance.
* ? Compose professional outreach messages and calendar invites (GMT) with agenda and consent notes.
* ?? Schedule an informational interview or submit a mini-case petition, uploading acceptable proof.
* ?? Maintain an Outreach Log to track communications and evidence.

?? HS1731 - Career Micro-Presentation Guide (2–3 minutes)
* ? Title & Sector: Role name and industry (5–10 sec)
* ? Why this career? Motivation & context in Ghana (20–30 sec)
* ? Key duties & H&S relevance: Main safety tasks, common hazards (40–60 sec)
* ? Qualifications & CPD route: Entry credentials, NEBOSH, local accreditations (20–30 sec)
* ? Practitioner question: One targeted question to ask interviewee (15–20 sec)
Tip: Keep slides/notes minimal — 1–2 bullets per heading.

?? EC1732 - Outreach & Scheduling — Best Practices
* ? Offer 3 proposed times (GMT) in first message.
* ? Keep initial message ?5 sentences; state purpose & duration (20 min).
* ? Provide a calendar invite with timezone, meeting link, agenda (3 bullets), and consent note.
* ? Use professional signature with name, Buzzjuice Courses programme, phone, and LinkedIn.
* ?? Outreach Log (required): 
DateContact NameRoleMethodOutcomeEvidence File Name

?? EC1734 - Calendar Invite Essentials
* Title: Informational Interview — [Role] (Buzzjuice Courses)
* Time & Duration (GMT) + timezone label
* Meeting link (Google Meet) or phone number
* Brief agenda (3 bullets)
* Consent line: “This meeting may be recorded for educational use with your permission.”
* Contact info for rescheduling

? EC1735 - Evidence Rules — Acceptable Proof
    (choose one)
* ? Signed/typed email from interviewee (official letterhead or verified account)
* ? LinkedIn thread screenshot (with timestamp & identity)
* ? Calendar invite screenshot or .ics file with meeting URL
* ? 30–90 sec audio/video clip introducing host
* ? Photo of student + interviewee with signed consent form
Note: Identity of interviewee must be verifiable; personal contact info may be blurred.

? HS1739 - Discussion Quiz & Resources
1. List three acceptable interview scheduling evidence types.
2. Why offer 3 time slots in your first outreach message? Give two benefits.
3. What three items must a calendar invite include to be acceptable evidence?
4. How would you respond if the interviewee asks for questions in advance? (brief outline)
5. Name two advantages of joining a Career Club pod for EC1700.

?? Practical Tips
* Prepare one-page Career Snapshot to attach with outreach (role + 3 focused questions).
* Keep timestamps & screenshots; export calendar invites as .ics files.
* Use LinkedIn when email fails — short, polite messages often get faster responses.
* Always document consent for recordings.
* If no practitioner is available after 3 attempts, submit a mini-case petition.

?? Mini-Case Petition
Submit after 3 documented outreach attempts:
* Proposed sector & learning outcomes
* Rationale & suggested practitioner sources
* Instructor petition request for approval
?? Deliverables & Micro-Checkpoint
* Upload proof of scheduled interview OR mini-case petition + outreach log.
* Optional: upload 2–3 min micro-presentation recording for peer feedback.
