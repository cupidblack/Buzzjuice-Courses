HS1330 – Verbal Communication & Impactful Presentation Skills 
?? Learning Outcomes
By the end of this lesson, learners will be able to:
? Craft clear, concise verbal messages for diverse workplace audiences.
? Deliver structured, engaging public presentations that inform and inspire.
? Manage stage fright and build confidence using practical techniques.
? Deliver persuasive presentations that influence safe behaviour and teamwork.

?? HS1331 - Crafting Clear, Concise & Compelling Verbal Messages
(?? Focus on clarity, tone, and simplicity)
?? One key idea per message – short and focused.
?? Use active voice: “Report all incidents immediately.”
?? Avoid jargon – explain terms for mixed literacy audiences.
?? Support key points with relatable examples in local languages (Twi, Ga, Ewe).
?? End with a single actionable takeaway.
?? Video: Julian Treasure – How to Speak So That People Want to Listen (TED) (9 min, verified Nov 12 2025)

?? HS1332 - Structure, Delivery & Audience Engagement
Three-Part Presentation Formula:
1?? Opening (Hook): Story, quote, or question.
2?? Body (Message): 3 key points or examples.
3?? Conclusion (Action): Clear next step or safety behaviour.
Delivery Tips:
?? Maintain audience contact.
?? Use vocal variety and pacing for interest.
? Purposeful gestures and confident stance.
?? Simple slides – one idea per slide, large text and icons.
?? Ask quick questions to involve your audience.
?? Video: Chris Anderson – TED’s Secret to Great Public Speaking (TED) (8 min, verified)


?? HS1333 - Managing Stage Fright & Building Confidence
Common Symptoms: Nervous voice, sweaty palms, racing thoughts.
2-Minute Confidence Routine:
1?? Breathe deeply (4 in – 4 hold – 4 out).
2?? Power posture (stand tall, open chest).
3?? Say your first line aloud before starting.
Mindset: Focus on the message, not on yourself.
Start small – practise with friends or mirror before formal sessions.
?? Video: TED-Ed – The Science of Stage Fright & How to Overcome It (4 min, verified)

?? HS1334 - Delivering Persuasive Presentations
Goal: Influence, inform, and inspire action through clarity and emotion.
Persuasion Model (IRA):
* Inform: Share short factual data.
* Relate: Link to personal or community value (“Your safety saves lives.”).
* Act: Give one specific, measurable action (“Check PPE before work starts.”).
?? Video: ILO – Workplace Safety: Effective Communication (6 min, verified)




?? HS1335 - Case Study: Tema Port — One Clear Message Prevents a Near-Miss
Scenario:
At Tema Port, a contractor almost began maintenance before confirming lockout.
A safety officer simplified his briefing: “Confirm lockout before cleaning.”
He included a 30-second story and teach-back question.
? Result: Two near-misses were avoided the following week.
Key Takeaways:
* Simplicity ensures better recall.
* Real stories make messages relatable.
* One clear action = safer outcomes.
