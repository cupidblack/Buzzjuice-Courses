?? Lesson 2 — Subscriptions & Pricing
Lesson Introduction
Welcome to Lesson 2 of the Registration & Orientation program.
In this lesson, you will learn how Buzzjuice subscriptions work, why they are required for access to courses, and how to manage payments and renewals.
Your subscription is not just a fee — it is your registration key that unlocks course access, keeps your account active, and ensures you remain in good standing. Understanding the subscription tiers and rules will help you avoid interruptions in your learning journey.
________________________________________
?? Topic 1: Why Subscriptions Are Required
•	All Buzzjuice Courses operate on a subscription model.
•	The subscription covers your registration fee and enables access to exclusive learning content.
•	Without an active subscription, you will not be able to:
o	Take the Registration & Orientation final assessment.
o	Access Health & Safety course quizzes or graded activities.
o	Earn certificates or badges.
?? Key Point: An active subscription is the foundation of your registration.
________________________________________
?? Topic 2: Subscription Plans & Pricing
Buzzjuice offers four subscription options. You can choose the plan that best suits your study schedule:
Plan	Duration	Price	Cost per Month (approx.)
Monthly	27 days	27 GHC	27 GHC
Quarterly	3 months (90 days)	50 GHC	~16.6 GHC
Semi-Annual	6 months (180 days)	84 GHC	14 GHC
Annual	12 months (365 days)	150 GHC	12.5 GHC
•	Longer plans offer better value per month.
•	You may upgrade, downgrade, or renew your plan at any time from your Account > Subscriptions page.
•	Payments are securely processed through WooCommerce Subscriptions.
?? Tip: Choose a longer plan if you want uninterrupted access and lower monthly cost.
________________________________________
?? Topic 3: Payment Rules & Renewals

•	Failed payments: If your payment fails, your account will temporarily lose access until payment is resolved.
•	Renewal reminders: Emails will be sent 7 days and 1 day before your renewal date.
•	Cancellation: Cancelling a subscription ends access at the end of your current billing period.
?? Important: Losing your subscription mid-course will immediately block access to Health & Safety content until you renew.
________________________________________
?? Topic 4: Subscription + Orientation Rules
•	To attempt the Registration & Orientation final quiz, you must have an active subscription.
•	Completing orientation gives you the “Oriented” role in your account.
•	To continue into Health & Safety courses, you must:
1.	Have an active subscription, and
2.	Pass the Orientation quiz with at least 70%.
?? Both subscription and orientation status are required for access.
________________________________________
?? Topic 5: Refunds & Support
•	Refund policy: Refunds are only available if you cancel within 7 days of your first subscription payment and have not yet attempted a course quiz.
•	Support: For billing issues, contact:
o	Email: support@buzzjuice.net
o	Support Page: [Buzzjuice Support Portal] (link placeholder)

?? LearnDash Lesson: Lesson 3 — Access Rules & Gating
Lesson Title:
Access Rules & Gating
Lesson Introduction:
Welcome to Lesson 3 of the Buzzjuice Registration & Orientation program.
In this lesson, you’ll learn how Buzzjuice Courses manage access to Health & Safety content using subscription plans, orientation status, and gating rules. These rules ensure fairness, protect course integrity, and guarantee that only prepared learners access exclusive material.
By the end of this lesson, you will:
•	Understand which courses require a subscription.
•	Know what the “exclusive-content” tag means.
•	Recognize why Orientation completion (Oriented status) is required.
•	Learn what happens if a subscription expires or lapses mid-course.
•	Be able to explain the steps needed to restore access after expiry.
________________________________________
Topic 1: Why Gating Exists
Content:
Buzzjuice Courses use gating rules to make sure that learners are properly prepared and have active subscriptions before accessing exclusive Health & Safety content.
Gating exists for three main reasons:
1.	Fair access — Only subscribers who support the platform financially can access advanced training.
2.	Quality assurance — Orientation ensures you understand the rules before starting Health & Safety courses.
3.	Integrity — Prevents users without registration or expired orientation from bypassing requirements.
Think of gating as a secure doorway: only learners with the right keys (subscription + orientation pass) can enter.
________________________________________
Topic 2: The Role of Subscriptions
Content:
To access any exclusive course, lesson, or quiz, you must hold an active subscription.
?? Buzzjuice subscription plans:
•	27 GHC per month
•	50 GHC every 3 months
•	84 GHC every 6 months
•	150 GHC every 12 months
If your subscription expires:
•	You will be redirected automatically to the subscription purchase page when trying to access exclusive content.
•	You must renew to continue learning.
________________________________________
Topic 3: The Exclusive-Content Tag
Content:
Certain courses, lessons, and quizzes are labeled with the “exclusive-content” tag.
This means:
•	The system checks for your subscription status before loading the page.
•	If your subscription is inactive, you’ll be redirected to the subscription purchase page.
•	If your subscription is active but orientation is incomplete, you’ll be prompted to finish Orientation first.
Example:
If you try to access a Health & Safety quiz without an active subscription, you’ll be redirected to the subscription page.
________________________________________
Topic 4: Orientation and Oriented Status
Content:
Completing Orientation is just as important as holding a subscription.
•	Once you pass the Orientation final assessment, you’ll receive the “Oriented” role.
•	This role acts as a second key, unlocking Health & Safety content alongside your subscription.
•	Without Orientation, you cannot progress into the Health & Safety Diploma, even if your subscription is active.
________________________________________
Topic 5: Losing Access
Content:
There are two common reasons learners lose access:
1.	Subscription expires — you will be redirected to renew.
2.	Orientation expires — after 152 days, your Orientation status lapses, and all course data is deleted.
To restore access:
•	Renew your subscription (if expired).
•	Re-complete Orientation to regain Oriented status.
This ensures all learners remain up-to-date and compliant with Buzzjuice policies.
________________________________________
Topic 6: Key Takeaways
Content (bullet points):
•	Access to Health & Safety courses is gated by subscription + orientation.
•	The exclusive-content tag enforces these rules automatically.
•	Orientation completion = Oriented role, required for further progress.
•	Losing access = renew subscription + redo Orientation (if expired).
•	Gating protects course integrity and ensures a fair learning environment.
________________________________________
? End of Lesson 3: Access Rules & Gating ?

?? LearnDash Lesson Module: Lesson 4 — Expiry, Retention & Re-registration
Lesson Introduction
Welcome to Lesson 4: Expiry, Retention & Re-registration.
Every learner’s Registration & Orientation (R&O) status on Buzzjuice Courses is valid for 152 days. This rule ensures that learners remain active, compliant with platform policies, and up to date with subscription requirements.
In this lesson, you will learn:
•	How long your orientation status lasts.
•	What happens when the 152-day limit is reached.
•	How your data and course progress are managed.
•	The steps to re-register and continue your studies.
By the end of this lesson, you’ll understand the importance of renewing your subscription on time and how to maintain uninterrupted access to Health & Safety course content.
________________________________________
Topic 1: Orientation Expiry (152 Days)
•	Your Registration & Orientation completion is valid for 152 days from the date you pass the final assessment.
•	After 152 days, your R&O status expires, and your access to Health & Safety courses will be restricted until you renew.
•	The expiry countdown begins immediately after passing the orientation assessment, not at the point of subscription renewal.
Key Point: Expiry is tied to your orientation completion, not just your subscription.
________________________________________
Topic 2: Data Retention & Deletion
•	Once the 152-day period ends, all course and user statistics connected to your Registration & Orientation are deleted.
•	Deleted data includes:
o	Course progress records
o	Lesson completions
o	Quiz attempts and scores
o	Orientation certificate and badge
•	This ensures compliance with platform policies and creates a fair reset for all learners.
Note: Personal details stored in your BuddyBoss profile (e.g., education history, emergency contact, health information) remain intact and are not deleted.
________________________________________
Topic 3: Renewal & Re-registration
To regain full access after expiry, you must:
1.	Maintain an active subscription (renew your subscription plan if expired).
2.	Re-complete the Registration & Orientation course (all lessons + final assessment).
3.	Pass the orientation quiz again to restore your “Oriented” status.
Only then will your access to Health & Safety courses be unlocked.
________________________________________
Topic 4: Reminder Notifications
•	You will receive automated reminder emails before your 152-day orientation expiry.
•	Reminders are typically sent at: 30, 14, 7, 3, and 1 day(s) before expiry.
•	These notifications help you renew on time and avoid losing your course progress.
Tip: Add support@buzzjuice.net to your safe senders list so you don’t miss these reminders.
________________________________________
Topic 5: Importance of Staying Current
Why does Buzzjuice enforce expiry and renewal?
•	To keep learners engaged and accountable.
•	To make sure learners always know the latest platform rules and subscription policies.
•	To ensure compliance with the Health & Safety diploma structure.
This process supports a fair learning environment and strengthens the credibility of Buzzjuice certifications.
________________________________________
Lesson Summary
•	Orientation status is valid for 152 days.
•	After expiry, course data is deleted.
•	Re-registration requires a valid subscription + retaking orientation.
•	Reminder emails help you act before your expiry date.
•	Expiry rules keep the learning community fair, current, and compliant.
________________________________________
? End of Lesson 4 — Expiry, Retention & Re-registration

?? LearnDash Lesson Module — Lesson 5
Title: Academy Bylaws, Conduct & Assessment Rules
________________________________________
Lesson Introduction
Welcome to Lesson 5 of the Registration & Orientation program.
In this lesson, you will become familiar with the Academy Bylaws, conduct expectations, and assessment rules that govern your learning experience on the Buzzjuice Courses platform.
Every student is expected to maintain a standard of professionalism, respect, and accountability while taking part in any course or activity. The bylaws outline the terms, conditions, privacy policies, rules, and procedures that apply to all members.
Understanding and agreeing to these guidelines is a requirement before continuing to Health & Safety courses and other exclusive-content programs.
By the end of this lesson, you should:
•	Recognize the key categories of the Buzzjuice Academy Bylaws
•	Understand your responsibilities as a registered learner
•	Know the consequences of misconduct, plagiarism, or dishonesty in assessments
•	Be prepared to acknowledge compliance with bylaws before assessments
________________________________________
Topic 1: The Academy Bylaws Overview
The Buzzjuice Academy operates under a set of bylaws that provide the foundation for fair participation and safe learning.
These bylaws are based on the Koware Bylaws and apply to all courses, forums, clubs, and groups.
Categories of Bylaws include:
1.	Terms & Conditions – your rights and responsibilities as a learner.
2.	Rules & Regulations – platform use, participation in forums/groups, and academic standards.
3.	Policies & Procedures – guidance for assessments, appeals, and complaints.
4.	Privacy – data protection and confidentiality of student records.
5.	Torah Bylaws – included for reference and study; not subject to amendment.
All members are expected to review the bylaws regularly. Amendments (except for Torah Bylaws) are voted on and approved by the Academy’s governing board.
?? Action Point: Access the full bylaws in the Academy’s forum: [Bylaws & Policies Group Link]
________________________________________
Topic 2: Conduct Expectations
To maintain a positive and professional environment, all learners are expected to follow these standards of conduct:
•	Respect: Treat fellow students, instructors, and staff with courtesy in all interactions.
•	Integrity: No cheating, plagiarism, or use of unauthorized resources in assessments.
•	Collaboration: Group work and discussions must remain constructive and supportive.
•	Compliance: Adhere to Academy rules in forums, clubs, and study groups.
•	Responsibility: Creators of clubs or groups are accountable for member conduct within their group.
?? Consequences for Misconduct
•	Temporary suspension of account or course access
•	Removal from groups or clubs
•	Permanent ban in cases of repeated or serious violations
________________________________________
Topic 3: Assessment Rules
Assessments are designed to ensure you have understood and can apply the concepts presented. To keep the evaluation fair and meaningful:
1.	Eligibility
o	Only students with an active subscription and completed Orientation can access assessments.
2.	Assessment Requirements
o	Final Orientation Quiz: 20 questions, passing grade of 70% (14/20).
o	Health & Safety assessments: some lessons are locked until you pass the required quiz.
3.	Assessment Conduct
o	Work must be completed individually unless otherwise stated.
o	Sharing answers or using unauthorized materials is considered misconduct.
4.	Retake Policy
o	Orientation quiz: up to 3 retakes allowed.
o	After 3 failed attempts, you must contact support for review.
5.	Certification & Records
o	On passing Orientation, you will be granted the Oriented role, a certificate, and a badge.
o	Orientation expires in 152 days; progress and records are then deleted. You must re-register and repeat Orientation to regain status.
________________________________________
Lesson Summary
In this lesson, you learned:
•	The categories of the Academy Bylaws (Terms, Conditions, Rules, Regulations, Policies, Procedures, Torah Bylaws).
•	Your responsibilities for respectful and professional conduct.
•	Key assessment rules, including eligibility, honesty, passing requirements, and retake policies.
Compliance with these bylaws and rules ensures a fair, safe, and professional environment for all learners on the Buzzjuice platform.

When learning online, it’s important to know where to get help. Buzzjuice provides multiple support channels to ensure you never feel stuck.
Available Support Options:
•	Email Support: Contact the team at support@buzzjuice.net for technical or billing questions.
•	BuddyBoss Groups: Join the “Support & Help” group for community-based troubleshooting and peer support.

After successfully completing Orientation, your account will be updated to reflect your new status.

If you don’t pass Orientation the first time, don’t worry—retakes are allowed.
•	You have up to 3 attempts to pass the final Orientation quiz.
•	After each failed attempt, you’ll be directed back to key lessons for review.
•	If you do not pass after 3 tries, you’ll need to open a support ticket for manual assistance.
This ensures that every learner entering the Health & Safety program is truly prepared.

2.	Select HS1000 — Fundamentals of Health & Safety.
